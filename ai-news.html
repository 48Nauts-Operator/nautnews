MIT Develops Programmable Proteins to Modify DNA in Human Cells

MIT researchers have discovered a new class of programmable DNA modifying systems called OMEGAs (Obligate Mobile Element Guided Activity). These ancient DNA-cutting enzymes, guided by small pieces of RNA, may naturally be involved in shuffling small bits of DNA throughout bacterial genomes. While they originated in bacteria, they have now been engineered to work in human cells, opening new possibilities for gene therapy and genetic research.
